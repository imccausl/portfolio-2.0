Details of The Last 7 Days

I am currently reading JavaScript with Promises by Daniel Parker (O'Reilly Press, 2015).

I love trying to understand how things work. In fact, I built this site entirely with vanilla JavaScript instead of using any of the abundance of libraries that would have expedited the process of creating routers because I wanted to understand what goes into the creation of routing libraries. A lot of my practice projects are like that, too. I want to understand the fundamentals before I reap the benefits of the magic.

Before I threw myself into coding, I had aspirations of becoming an academic. I completed a Master's degree and was six years into a PhD (admittedly, 4 of those years I was floundering because I knew I didn't want to devote myself to academia anymore, but I didn't know how to change the course that I had set myself to for so long). Strangely enough, during this period, my research became more-and-more centered around understanding the transmission and reception of information: in particular, how a string of bytes can come to be translated into something intelligable for human beings.
